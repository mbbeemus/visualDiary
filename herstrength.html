HerStrength

Menstrual Cycle App Synced with Exercising Routines

Project Overview

HerStrength is a menstrual cycle tracking app synced with a woman’s exercising routine to promote healthy workouts that align with each phase of a woman’s cycle. This app takes into account a woman’s menstrual cycle, noting what days she is in her luteal phase, menstruation, etc. Concurrently, the app pulls in data about women’s health to provide suggestions for workout routines. For example, in the follicular phase is a time of great energy where women’ s strength is usually at an all time high. At this point, she should focus on muscle building and weight lifting. On the other hand, during the luteal phase, a woman should focus on rest and lower-intensity to prepare her body for menstruation. HerStrength’s goal is to promote women’s health for a lifestyle filled with strength and confidence.

Research

The intended demographic of this app are younger women ranging from the ages of 18-29. These women likely promote a healthy lifestyle for themselves or are looking to begin a more-healthy approach to living. These women are often very busy and may not have the time to complete their own research about their cycles. The process of logging data cannot be exhaustive. In order to ensure these women stay consistent on the app, logging their workouts should be an optional feature and not complex if they do decide to use it.

Pain Points

Hormone Fluctuations

  • A lot of these women are likely frustrated with the fluctuation of hormones throughout the cycle which may cause inconsistencies in the gym.
  • These fluctuations can be very frustrating and discouraging.
  • Women may feel like they are not improving or even falling behind but instead, their strength and energy is actually linked with their cycle.

Lack of Clear Guidlines

  • Many workout apps are focused on the male physique and do not take into account the varying energy/hormone levels for women throughout the month.
  • Many women are told, “rest more” or “exercise more” to help with their health- but never really told why.
  • Workout apps do not give guidelines to women to optimize on the menstrual cycle.
  • Many cycle tracking apps do not integrate physical health but instead, focus on mental health/mood.
